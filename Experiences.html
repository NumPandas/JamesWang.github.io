Research

  • Sextupole Alignment

    • Utilized the Tao program in the BMAD particle accelerator simulator library to explore a 3-kicker-bump based method to align sextupole families. Found potential for the method to provide precise estimates of sextupole misalignments. Performed data analysis with the NumPy library in Python. Also assisted in collection and formatting of beam-data used to measure beam sizes. Under the supervision of Georg Hoffstaetter in the Hoffstaetter Group at Cornell University.

    • Beam-Based Alignment of Sextupole Families in the EIC

    • Beam-based alignment of individual members of sextupole families

    • Study of the systematic error contributions to the measurement of beam size using sextupole magnets

  • Machine Learning for Thin-Film Quantum Material

    • Used the NumPy and Tenserflow libraries in Python to configure and develop a neural network that constructs the electron distributions of thin-film interfaces of quantum materials. Conducted data analysis, parameter configuration, and incorporated noise into model in the form of gaussian machine noise and roughness factors. Processed experimental data for training and testing. Under the supervision of Ankit Disa in the Disa Group at Cornell University.

    • Unpublished final report available upon request

  • 2D Quantum Materials and Waveguides

    • Simulated ridge waveguides with using COMSOL Multiphysics to examine EM modes with and without the presence of monolayer graphe. Fabricated 2D Material device stacks of graphene and BN. Under the supervision of Andrew Pierce and Chirag Vaswani in the Mak-Shan Group.
